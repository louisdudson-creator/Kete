About Kete

Kete is a calm, practical mental health skills and wellbeing website for people in Aotearoa New Zealand.

It is designed to help you find one useful next step when things feel hard, overwhelming, stuck, stressful, or unclear.

Kete offers simple pathways, short skills, and printable handouts. You can use it on your own, with trusted whānau, family, a support person, or alongside a clinician.

Kete is not here to label you. It is not organised around diagnoses. It is organised around everyday problems people recognise.

How Kete works: Kete is a simple information and skills website. It does not collect personal information, assess safety, monitor symptoms, or respond to emergencies. Use urgent services if there may be immediate danger.

The idea behind Kete

A kete is something that can hold useful things. Kete / Kete Ara is built around the same idea: a place to gather practical tools, small steps, and supportive guidance.

About the name and context: Kete uses some Aotearoa New Zealand language and context. It should do this carefully, plainly, and with respect. It is not a substitute for culturally specific support, kaupapa Māori services, local iwi or community support, clinical care, or emergency help.
Whānau can mean different thingsUse the people who are safe and right for you. That may be whānau, family, friends, community, or services.
Support should be safeNot every family or whānau relationship is helpful. Choose trusted people and get more help if safety is uncertain.
Use local help when neededIn New Zealand, call 111 for immediate danger and call or text 1737 if you need to talk with someone.

The aim is not to fix everything at once. The aim is to help you find one next step that feels possible.

How Kete is organised

Kete has four main kinds of help: pathways for choosing a direction, skills for trying one action, handouts for printing or saving, and Resources for finding NZ support options.

It works best when you keep it small: choose one page, use the part that helps, and move to support if things feel unsafe, urgent, or too hard to manage alone.

What Kete is and is not

Kete is a practical mental health skills and wellbeing website. It is here to help you find small, useful steps when life feels hard.

What Kete can help with

  • slow things down when you feel overwhelmed
  • try a grounding or breathing skill
  • choose one next step
  • print a worksheet or handout
  • prepare to ask for support
  • support someone else in a calm, practical, and safe way

What Kete cannot do

  • respond in an emergency
  • assess whether you are safe
  • diagnose a mental health condition
  • provide therapy
  • replace a clinician, GP, counsellor, crisis team, or emergency service
  • tell you what medication to take
  • provide personalised medical advice
  • monitor your symptoms
  • guarantee that a skill will work

Privacy

This privacy page is written for a simple static Kete website without accounts, forms, personal profiles, symptom tracking, stored user records, analytics, or a database.

Kete does not ask you to create an account, enter your name, enter personal health information, use a chat service, or store a clinical record.

Worksheets and handouts

Some Kete handouts include blank spaces where you can write notes. If you print or save a handout, you are responsible for where that copy is stored and who can see it.

Local browser storage

Accessibility

Accessibility: Kete uses labelled controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, keyboard-accessible cards, screen-reader status messages for search, and print-friendly handout styles.

Kete is intended to be calm, readable, mobile-friendly, and usable when people are distressed or tired. Accessibility is part of site safety.

Kete aims to be

  • easy to read
  • plain-language
  • mobile-friendly
  • keyboard-usable where possible
  • readable with good contrast
  • organised with clear headings
  • usable without relying on colour alone
  • printable where relevant
  • calm rather than visually overwhelming
Ongoing accessibility: Kete should keep being checked on mobile, keyboard, screen reader, and print settings. Feedback about readability, safety, or usability should be treated as important.

If something on Kete is hard to read, use, print, or understand, that should be treated as an improvement priority.

Kete should also avoid forcing disclosure, body focus, cultural assumptions, or completing worksheets perfectly. Users should be able to choose one small step and stop.
